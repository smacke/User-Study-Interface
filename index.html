Instructions

In this HIT, your goal is to estimate the visual similarity of charts, similar to the charts you may have seen in Excel, or published along with news articles. If you are not familiar with how to read charts like the one given alongside (showing Donald Trumpâ€™s popularity over time), please do not attempt this HIT.

The HIT for our task interface is outlined in the figure below, and contains three parts: a reference chart (block A), a collection of charts (block B) and a collection of rating buckets, numbered from 1--5 (block C).

Your goal is to compare each chart in the collection of charts with the reference chart, and place it in a rating bucket based on how similar the chart is to the reference chart. The higher the similarity, the higher the number of the rating bucket you should use (5 is most similar, 1 is least similar). You can place multiple charts in each bucket. Your task is complete when all of the charts in the collection of charts are placed in some bucket. After that you have answer several questions related to the task.

Now what do we mean by similar?

In the above figure (outlining the interface), there are two charts in the collection of charts, p and q. When comparing p to the reference chart, since it is identical to the reference, it is highly similar, and therefore we may want to place it in a higher rating bucket (closer to 5). On the other hand q shows inverse behavior to the reference -- the y axis is decreasing as the x axis increases, while for the reference the y axis is increasing as x axis increases. Therefore, we may want to place this chart in a lower rating bucket (closer to 1). Our goal is to understand what you find to be similar -- so how you decide to use these rating buckets to judge the similarity of the collection of charts is up to you.

Step by step instructions:

Please read the following instructions and then click OK to begin the task:

  1. Your goal is to drag each chart in the collection of charts to one of the rating buckets based on similarity with the refernce chart (5 for most similar, 1 for least similar).
  2. To inspect these charts closer, if you hover over them, they will be magnified.
  3. You need to drag all of the charts in the collection of charts to some bucket to complete this task. You can change your answer at any time by moving a chart from one bucket to another.
  4. Once you are done with dragging all of the charts to some bucket and you are satisfied with your answer, click the Continue button. You will not be able to make further changes to your answer, but you can still hover over charts to magnify them and inspect them closer.
  5. After you click Continue, several questions related to the task will appear. Once you answer all the questions, you can click Submit to finish the task.
  6. Whenever you want to see the instructions during your assignment, you can click the Instruction tab at the top. You can click it again to hide it.
